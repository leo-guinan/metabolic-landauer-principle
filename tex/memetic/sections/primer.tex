\section{Primer: Why This Meme Matters}

\subsection{Physics in Plain Language}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Landauer 101}: deleting one bit of information always costs energy. No exceptions.
  \item \textbf{Human version}: learning is compression; relief is deletion. Both burn and release calories.
  \item \textbf{Claim}: unresolved loops are informational junk drawers that your metabolism keeps funding with stress hormones and stored fat.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Core Vocabulary}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=0pt]
  \item[Loop] A thought, emotion, or decision you keep replaying.
  \item[Compression] Boiling the loop down to one true sentence.
  \item[Confidence] The moment you trust that sentence enough to act.
  \item[Deletion] An irreversible gesture that says ``this story is archived.''
\end{description}

\subsection{Audience Translation}
This document speaks to memetic experimenters: people who enjoy running protocols on themselves, logging data, and turning shareable stories into collective evidence.
